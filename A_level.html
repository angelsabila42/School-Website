Advanced level

As one of the top schools for Government admissions each year, we offer a diverse range of A-Level subjects under the current Ugandan curriculum, preparing students for any Arts or Science combination. Our well-structured program ensures that students qualify for professional courses at universities and institutions of higher learning.

We continuously review our subject offerings to match the evolving needs and abilities of our students, incorporating hands-on training to equip them both academic and practical skills. This commitment to excellence is overseen by our headmistress, who ensures that the school maintains high educational standards.

Subjects Offered in the Updated O-Level Curriculum

The revised curriculum includes a mix of compulsory and elective subjects:

Compulsory Subjects:

Focuses on developing proficiency in reading, writing, listening, and speaking. Emphasizes grammar, vocabulary, comprehension, composition, and critical analysis of texts.

UCE Examination Papers: Paper 1 (Composition and Comprehension), Paper 2 (Language Use and Summary).

Covers topics such as algebra, geometry, trigonometry, statistics, and calculus. Aims to develop logical reasoning, problem-solving skills, and numerical literacy.

UCE Examination Papers: Paper 1 (Pure Mathematics), Paper 2 (Applied Mathematics).

Explores the study of living organisms, including cell biology, genetics, ecology, human anatomy, and physiology. Encourages practical investigations and understanding of biological processes.

UCE Examination Papers: Paper 1 (Theory), Paper 2 (Practical)

Focuses on the composition, structure, properties, and changes of matter. Topics include atomic structure, chemical reactions, periodic table, and industrial processes. Practical laboratory work is integral.

UCE Examination Papers: Paper 1 (Theory), Paper 2 (Practical).

Studies the fundamental principles governing energy and matter. Covers mechanics, electricity, magnetism, waves, and modern physics concepts. Emphasizes analytical skills and experimental techniques.

UCE Examination Papers: Paper 1 (Theory), Paper 2 (Practical).

Examines physical and human environments. Topics include landforms, climate, population dynamics, and economic activities. Encourages spatial awareness and environmental stewardship..

UCE Examination Papers: Paper 1 (Physical Geography), Paper 2 (Human and Economic Geography), Paper 3 (Fieldwork).

Explores historical events, movements, and figures, both locally and globally. Emphasizes understanding political systems, governance, and civic responsibilities.

UCE Examination Papers: Paper 1 (East African History), Paper 2 (World History), Paper 3 (Political Education).

Offers insights into religious beliefs, practices, and ethics. Students can choose between Christian Religious Education (CRE) and Islamic Religious Education (IRE), fostering moral development and interfaith understanding.

UCE Examination Papers: Paper 1 and Paper 2, varying based on the chosen religious focus.
