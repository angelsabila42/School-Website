Ordinary level

The Ugandan O-Level curriculum is designed to provide students with a comprehensive education, preparing them for both Advanced Level studies and future professional courses at universities and other institutions of higher learning. The curriculum emphasizes both academic knowledge and practical skills, ensuring students are well-equipped for various career paths.

At the end of the four-year O-Level program, students sit for the Uganda Certificate of Education (UCE) examinations, administered by the Uganda National Examinations Board (UNEB).

Subjects Offered in the Updated O-Level Curriculum

The revised curriculum includes a mix of compulsory and elective subjects:

Compulsory Subjects:

UCE Examination Papers: Paper 1 (Composition and Comprehension), Paper 2 (Language Use and Summary).

UCE Examination Papers: Paper 1 (Theory), Paper 2 (Practical).
